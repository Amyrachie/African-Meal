UGBA: THE HEART OF TRADITIONAL IGBO MEALS

Ugba, also called Ukpaka, is a Nigerian indigenous food ingredient. Although it is used as an ingredient by several ethnic groups in Nigeria, it is a common ingredient among the Igbo’s in the Eastern part of Nigeria. It goes by several names including ukana by Efik people and Apara by the Yoruba tribe. Ugba is a protein-rich food that is got by solid state fermentation of seeds of African oil bean (Pentaclethra macrophylla) seed. With a short shelf life of about 3-5 days, Ugba is a basic food item used in cooking and preparing certain ceremonial traditional dishes. In Igbo land, ugba is used to make Abacha (also known as African salad), and mixed with cooked stock fish, to mention a few.

List of Ingredients Needed to Prepare UGBA

S/N Ingredients Measurements
1 Ugba 5cups
2 Palm Oil 450g or 1lb
3 Liquid Potash (Potassium Carbonate — Dissolved In water) A little bit
4 Hot Pepper Desired Amount
5 Sliced Crayfish Desired Amount
6 Kpomo, Stockfish, Meat Desired Amount
7 Salt To Taste

List of Steps/Methods to Prepare UGBA

  • Get a clean empty pot and pour palm oil in it. If the oil needs to be melted, do that and take off the stove once melted.
  • Slowly pour in the edible potash solution into the palm oil while stirring at the same time. Take note of the palm oil gradually changing colour and becoming thicker.
  • Add your dissolved ogiri.
  • Next comes the ground crayfish. Pour in the pieces of medium crayfish, stockfish and kpomo and stir all till nicely mixed. If using smoked fish, add it before stirring.
  • Transfer the pot/pan to medium heat.
  • Add the ugba/ukpaka, sliced pepper and the stock from kpomo,and stock fish.
  • Stir well and once heated, add salt to taste and it’s ready to serve. This meal can be garnished with fresh onions and garden egg leaves. Also paired nicely beside the dish is the traditional brew palmwine or your preferred drink.
